Overview

The Maui County Food Security Data Visualization Project is a partnership between Maui United Way, the Responsible Markets (Maui ESG Project), University of Hawaii, and NASA Acres/NASA Harvest, funded by NASA. It aims to address critical data gaps in Maui County related to food insecurity and climate change with satellite imagery and machine learning.

Satellite data also allows us to better understand the evolution and impact of the wildfires on Maui (and in the future, how to anticipate or prevent them). There is an abundance of information online about the fires including satellite data, but it can be hard to sort through everything. On this page, we organize the sources of satellite data related to the fires and share resources for accessing this satellite data and derived products.

Building Damage

Microsoft AI for Good damaged buildings visualization

The Microsoft AI for Good geospatial team used machine learning and very high resolution satellite imagery from Planet Inc's SkySat satellite constellation to identify damaged buildings in Lahaina following the wildfires. You can use their viewer tool to explore before and after satellite images of Lahaina and see the detections of building damage levels.
